Case study - DXU

DXU

Project : Call scheduling application Design

Project Brief
Back to Project
Context

DXU is Dashboard interface created for scheduling meeting between clients. The system automatically assigns calls, syncs with internal calendars, and sends reminders to both parties. Missed appointments are reduced through automated follow-ups and rescheduling options.

Role
  • UI/ UX Design
  • User Research

The Challenge

In the absence of a centralized call center application, managing customer calls, internal meetings, and issue tracking becomes increasingly inefficient and prone to errors. Without a unified platform, teams often rely on fragmented tools, leading to communication breakdowns, delayed responses, and inconsistent customer experiences.

The Design Solution

To address this gap, A dedicated call center application streamlines call handling, schedules and tracks meetings, and provides a structured system for logging and resolving problems. This not only enhances productivity and collaboration among team members but also ensures a higher standard of service delivery, accountability, and operational transparency.

Project Overview

  • 14 Weeks Timeline
  • 20 Pages
  • Figma, Adobe Tools
  • Windows Desktop Application

01 Discover Phase

Customer Research

In DXU the target users of a call center application typically include:

  • Call Center Agents – Primary users who handle inbound and outbound calls, requiring fast navigation, clear call controls, and minimal cognitive load.
  • Supervisors / Team Leads – Monitor live calls, agent performance, and queues, needing dashboards, alerts, and real-time insights.
  • Call Center Managers – Analyze trends, KPIs, and operational efficiency through reports and analytics.
  • Quality Assurance (QA) Analysts – Review call recordings, score interactions, and track compliance.
  • System Administrators / IT Staff – Configure users, integrations, security, and system settings.

Competitive analysis

We found out our 2 main competitors and did a competitive analysis to assess the strengths and weaknesses of current and potential competitors and build a strategy to identify potential opportunities where can design better application that cater user needs.

Key inputs derived from survey

Key inputs from a call center survey include customer satisfaction scores, call wait time and resolution feedback, agent professionalism and communication ratings, issue type and resolution status, and open-ended customer comments for improvement insights.

05 Testing Phase

Usability Testing

Usability testing in a call center application is critical because it ensures that agents, supervisors, and managers can efficiently navigate the system, access schedules, manage shifts, and handle calls without confusion or errors.

  • Users should be able to view their assigned shifts and breaks clearly without additional guidance.
  • The process of requesting time off is intuitive and easy to complete.
  • Users can quickly understand and navigate the scheduling interface.
  • Making changes to a shift (swap, overtime, or availability) requires minimal steps.
  • Schedule updates and notifications are clear and timely.
  • The calendar view makes it easy to identify working hours and non-working days.
  • Users can easily find information related to attendance and adherence.
  • The application responds quickly when loading schedules or submitting requests.
