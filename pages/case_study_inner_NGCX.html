Case study - NGCX

NGCX

Project : NEXT Generation Wealth Account Application Design

Project Brief
Back to Project
Context

NGCX wealth accound application is Dashboard cum mobile interface created for wealth account investment management for clients. Led the design and development of a highly interactive, rich user interface for insurance account management, focused on delivering a personalized and seamless onboarding experience for stakeholders. The enhanced UI significantly improved usability, driving higher engagement and simplified navigation across account features.

Role
  • UI/ UX Design
  • User Research

The Challenge

The existing wealth account management UI feels cluttered, with too many elements competing for attention on a single screen. Navigation is confusing because key actions like portfolio overview and transactions are buried in multiple menus. Important financial data lacks visual hierarchy, making it hard to quickly identify investments, performance, and risks.

The Design Solution

To address this gap, Spearheaded the end-to-end upgrade of an online insurance wealth account portal aimed at enhancing stakeholder user experience through a modernized, secure, and intuitive interface. Directed all phases of system implementation including requirements elicitation, project task breakdown, and timeline management to ensure on-time delivery. .

01 Discover Phase

Customer Research

The targeted users of a NGCXwealth account application typically include the following groups::

  • High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNWIs) – Users with significant assets who need portfolio tracking, investment insights, and personalized wealth management.
  • Affluent Professionals – Salaried or self-employed professionals looking to grow savings through investments, retirement planning, and tax optimization.
  • Retail Investors – Individuals managing mutual funds, stocks, ETFs, or bonds with basic to advanced tracking needs.
  • Business Owners & Entrepreneurs – Users managing personal and business wealth, cash flow, and long-term financial planning.
  • Financial Advisors / Relationship Managers – Professionals who manage and monitor client portfolios through advisor dashboards.

Competitive analysis

We found out our 2 main competitors and did a competitive analysis to assess the strengths and weaknesses of current and potential competitors and build a strategy to identify potential opportunities where can design better application that cater user needs.

Key inputs derived from survey

Key inputs from a this application survey include User Demographics & Profile, Account & Portfolio Usage, User Experience & Interface Feedback,Investment & Financial Insights.

05 Testing Phase

Usability Testing

Wealth apps are often complex, with multiple accounts, investments, and reports. Usability testing ensures users can find critical features—like portfolio overview, transactions, or goal tracking—without confusion or frustration.Users perform important actions such as investing, transferring funds, or generating reports. Testing identifies bottlenecks or unnecessary steps, helping users complete tasks quickly and accurately.

  • Evaluate ease of navigation: Can users find key features like portfolio overview, transactions, and reports?.
  • Assess clarity of information: Are balances, performance metrics, and investment summaries understandable?
  • Check efficiency of task completion: How quickly can users perform critical actions like transferring funds, setting goals, or reviewing investments?
  • Identify pain points: Detect confusing flows, unclear labels, or cluttered dashboards.
  • Measure satisfaction: Gauge overall user confidence and trust in the application.
  • A/B Testing: Test alternative UI layouts or dashboard designs.
  • Moderated Usability Testing: Observe participants completing tasks in real time.
