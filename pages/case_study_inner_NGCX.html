Case study - NGCX

Project Brief

Spearheaded the end-to-end UX upgrade of an online wealth account portal. Modernized mobile/web interfaces to streamline stakeholder workflows for 200K+ enterprise users.

Context

Next Generation Client Experience (NGCX) is a wealth management platform designed to help clients monitor their investment portfolios, track fund performance, and manage wealth accounts through a unified desktop and mobile experience.

The project focused on transforming an existing legacy application into a modern, intuitive digital platform that delivers a seamless and personalized user experience. The redesigned application introduced interactive dashboards, rich portfolio visualizations, and simplified navigation, enabling clients to access their financial information with greater clarity and confidence.

Project Overview

Duration

34 Weeks

Platform

Desktop and Mobile Application

Roles

Senior UX Designer

Tools

Figma, Adobe Creative Suite, Visual Studio

Deliverables

UX Research, UX Flows, IA, Wireframes, Interactive prototypes, High fidelity designs

The Challenge

The existing wealth management application was built on a legacy platform that no longer aligned with evolving user expectations.

Clients experienced difficulties navigating the application due to a cluttered interface, inconsistent information architecture, and limited visual representation of financial data. Key tasks such as reviewing portfolio performance, accessing investment details, and managing wealth accounts required multiple navigation steps, resulting in a fragmented user experience.

The business needed a modern digital solution that would simplify wealth management while improving usability, engagement, and overall client satisfaction.

The Design Solution

The NGCX initiative focused on redesigning the entire client experience by adopting a user-centered design approach.

The solution introduced a modern dashboard that provides clients with a comprehensive overview of their investments through interactive charts, portfolio summaries, and performance insights. Navigation was restructured to reduce complexity, while the interface was refreshed with a clean visual hierarchy that improves readability and accessibility.

The application was designed to support both desktop and mobile platforms, ensuring a consistent experience across devices while making wealth management simpler, faster, and more intuitive.

The Work process

Breaking down the project in to five key phases

01 Discover Phase
  • Customer Research

    To better understand user behaviors, investment goals, and pain points, user research was conducted with key stakeholder groups involved in wealth management.

    The primary user groups included:

    • High-Net-Worth Individuals managing diverse investment portfolios.
    • Affluent professionals focused on long-term wealth creation and retirement planning.
    • Retail investors actively monitoring mutual funds, stocks, ETFs, and other investment products.
    • Business owners managing both personal and business financial assets.
    • Financial Advisors and Relationship Managers responsible for monitoring and supporting client portfolios.

  • Competitive analysis

    A competitive analysis was conducted to evaluate leading wealth management platforms and understand current industry standards for digital investment experiences.

    The analysis focused on areas such as:

    • Dashboard experience & Investment tracking
    • Portfolio visualization.
    • Navigation patterns.
    • Reporting capabilities.
    • User engagement.
    • Mobile responsiveness.
  • Key inputs derived from survey

    The research identified several recurring themes across user interviews and stakeholder discussions.

    Users wanted:

    • A simplified dashboard that highlights key portfolio information.
    • Faster access to portfolio summaries and transaction history.
    • Personalized insights relevant to their financial goals.
    • A consistent experience across desktop and mobile devices.

    These insights became the foundation for the design strategy.

  • Market & Industry Research

    Understanding the wealth management landscape, emerging trends, and user expectations to identify opportunities and design a relevant digital wealth management experience.

    Research Areas Covered:

    • Analyzed current trends in digital wealth management and investment platforms.
    • Studied user expectations around portfolio visibility, transparency, and personalization.
    • Explored emerging technologies like AI-driven insights and automated advisory.

05 Testing Phase

Usability Testing

Usability testing was conducted to validate the redesigned experience and identify opportunities for further refinement before implementation.

Testing focused on evaluating how effectively users could complete common wealth management tasks while interacting with the new interface.

Testing Objectives :

  • Evaluate ease of navigation across key workflows.
  • Measure the clarity of financial information and portfolio summaries.
  • Assess efficiency in completing investment-related tasks./li>
  • Identify usability issues affecting task completion.
  • Measure overall user confidence and satisfaction.
  • Validate alternative dashboard layouts through A/B testing.
  • Gather qualitative feedback through moderated usability sessions.

The findings informed several iterative improvements, resulting in a more intuitive and user-friendly wealth management experience.
